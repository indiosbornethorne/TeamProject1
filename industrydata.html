Having looked at the Burning Glass Data, has your opinion of you ideal job changed? Why or why not?

Adam: My opinion towards the idea of working as a full stack developer has not changed, but ultimately, I am not certain as to what area of IT I would like to build a career around. Being in a role such as a full stack developer allows for a wholistic set of skills where the person needs to be able to do many different types of things and work with different technologies to create solutions. Although as seen in the Burning Glass data, there is less of a demand for full stack developers compared to other roles I would believe that the skills are highly transferrable and therefore, if I was skilled as full stack developer, I would easily be able to adapt and learn anything required to fulfil a role that was in high demand.

One of the high in demand roles was a front-end developer, being a full stack developer would mean that I would have the base knowledge to move into a front-end development role and then could undertake independent learning to become more specialized in the role. Some of the technologies listed that are trending being SQL or java for example, would be languages that a full stack developer has experience with, or with similar languages that would allow for smooth transition into the more popular technology. I feel working towards being a full stack developer is still a great career plan as businesses continue the trend of moving their operations onto computer systems and they will continue to embrace technology as a vital piece of their organizations. If I were to have skills in a wide range of areas, such as what is required by a full stack developer, I feel I would be secure for employment both presently and moving into the future as a reliance on artificial intelligence develops and processing power grows.
