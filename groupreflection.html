Group Reflection

As a group, we were extremely fortunate to find that our personalities meshed quite well together and that we had group members with the skills required to complete the project. Something that surprised most of our group was the ease at which we got along once we had broken the. The consensus among us is that we all worked quite well together as a unit and that discord was an excellent way to communicate with each other as it allowed 24/7 messaging and has message history and search options. The lack of conflict in our team contributed to a good group work experience for all members, some of whom are participating in their first group assignment and others whom have worked in teams before and have remarked on our exceptional cohesion.

Members have expressed that our group was well structured with clearly defined goals and that our communication was adequate. Some have suggested, however, that voice communication and a more defined meeting schedule may benefit our organisational skills as well as provide clearer direction and defined roles. Given this, there was some confusion early on as to who was doing what, but it was resolved by the appropriate delegation of tasks.

This group had to manage this assignment with full time work and other important life commitments and has done so admirably, always submitting work ahead of time and working hard to meet the requirements. The obvious exception is Liza. As has probably become apparent from our individual reflections, the consensus is that if she had not intended to complete the work, she should not have offered to take on responsibility. Nor should she have joined over a week into work on the assignment in the first place. If we must re-form or recruit new members for a future assignment, I think all of us will be wary of handing off important tasks to members who join late and/or are not as responsive as the others. In addition, for future group work, we have decided to keep a rolling bibliography rather than doing individual ones at the end of the project so that members can post information sources as they work. Not only does this ensure proper citations but will allow much faster sharing of information.

We all learned that it is possible to have a fun and enjoyable group work experience if the people involved are willing to put in the effort and be as personable as they can.
